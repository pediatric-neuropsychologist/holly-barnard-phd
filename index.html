What is Neuropsychology?

A neuropsychologist is an expert in the impact of atypical development (e.g., ASD, learning disorders, spina bifida), injury (e.g., Traumatic Bran Injury, birth trauma), disease (e.g., tumor, meningitis), and decline on the brain.

Patients can present with a number of complex conditions that, as they grow and mature, impact how they think, learn, and behave. A neuropsychologist helps patients, parents, and teachers to understand a person's brain and the way they function best, in case of:

  • Attention-Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder
  • Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)
  • Epilepsy
  • Genetic/Chromosomal Disorders
  • Hearing Loss
  • History of Brain Tumor
  • History of Neurologic Infections
  • Hydrocephalus
  • Metabolic Disorders
  • Neurodevelopmental Disorders
  • Oxygen Deprivation
  • Specific Learning Disorders
  • Structural Abnormalities of the Brain
  • Toxic Encephalopathy
  • Traumatic Brain Injury (TBI)
  • Vascular Events/Conditions
